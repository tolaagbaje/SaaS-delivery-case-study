## 🎯 Business Problem

The client's existing onboarding process was entirely manual — new users were guided through setup via email threads, PDFs, and phone calls. This created:

- **Long onboarding times** — averaging 5–7 days per new user
- **High support volume** — 60% of support tickets were onboarding-related
- **Poor user retention** — users who took longer to onboard were 3x more likely to churn
- **No visibility** — leadership had zero real-time data on onboarding progress

---

## ✅ Objectives

- [ ] Reduce user onboarding time by at least 40%
- [ ] Automate the top 5 manual onboarding steps
- [ ] Integrate onboarding tracking dashboard for leadership visibility
- [ ] Deliver within 8-week timeline with zero scope creep
- [ ] Achieve sign-off from 3 key stakeholders before launch

## 🗂️ Project Phases

### Phase 1 — Discovery & Scoping (Week 1)
- Conducted stakeholder interviews with Product, Engineering, and Customer Success leads
- Documented 23 functional requirements and 8 non-functional requirements
- Defined project scope, success criteria, and delivery milestones
- Identified 6 potential risks and documented mitigation strategies

### Phase 2 — Planning & Alignment (Week 2)
- Created full project plan with task breakdown across 4 sprints
- Assigned ownership across 9 team members
- Set up Jira board with epics, stories, and acceptance criteria
- Held project kickoff with all stakeholders

### Phase 3 — Sprint Execution (Weeks 3–7)
- **Sprint 1** — Core onboarding flow (user registration, profile setup)
- **Sprint 2** — Automated email sequences and in-app tooltips
- **Sprint 3** — Admin dashboard and progress tracking
- **Sprint 4** — QA, bug fixes, UAT, and final stakeholder review

### Phase 4 — Delivery & Review (Week 8)
- Coordinated final UAT session with client team
- Resolved 4 critical and 9 minor bugs before launch
- Deployed to production with zero downtime
- Conducted post-launch review and documentation handoff

## ⚠️ Risks & Mitigations

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|:---|:---|:---:|:---:|:---|
| 1 | Engineer availability during Sprint 3 | Medium | High | Cross-trained BA to support minor front-end tasks |
| 2 | Scope expansion from stakeholders | High | High | Weekly scope review meetings; change request process enforced |
| 3 | QA bottleneck before launch | Medium | High | Parallel testing introduced from Sprint 2 |
| 4 | Third-party API integration delay | Low | Medium | Mock API built as contingency in Sprint 1 |

## 💡 Key Learnings

1. **Front-load requirements** — The time invested in Week 1 discovery saved 3x more time during execution
2. **Parallel QA pays off** — Starting QA in Sprint 2 rather than Sprint 4 eliminated the traditional end-of-project crunch
3. **Stakeholder cadence matters** — Weekly touchpoints with all 3 stakeholders reduced change requests by keeping everyone aligned throughout
